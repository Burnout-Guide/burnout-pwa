Burnout Guide

Resources for recognizing and recovering from burnout

You're Not Alone

Burnout is a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged stress. It's more common than you think, and recovery is possible.

Understanding Burnout

Signs & Symptoms

  • Chronic exhaustion and fatigue
  • Cynicism and detachment
  • Feelings of ineffectiveness
  • Lack of accomplishment
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, insomnia)
  • Difficulty concentrating

Common Causes

  • Work overload
  • Lack of control
  • Insufficient rewards
  • Breakdown of community
  • Absence of fairness
  • Value conflicts

Recovery Steps

  • Acknowledge the burnout
  • Set boundaries
  • Prioritize self-care
  • Seek support
  • Consider professional help
  • Make gradual changes

Self-Care Strategies

Physical Well-being

  • Regular sleep schedule (7-9 hours)
  • Nutritious meals
  • Regular exercise
  • Time in nature
  • Breathing exercises

Mental Health

  • Mindfulness meditation
  • Journaling
  • Therapy or counseling
  • Support groups
  • Digital detox periods

Work-Life Balance

  • Clear work boundaries
  • Regular breaks
  • Learn to say "no"
  • Delegate when possible
  • Pursue hobbies

Professional Resources

When to Seek Help

Consider professional support if you experience:

  • Persistent feelings of hopelessness
  • Inability to perform daily tasks
  • Substance use concerns
  • Thoughts of self-harm
  • Physical health impacts

Remember

Recovery from burnout is a journey, not a destination. Be patient with yourself and celebrate small victories along the way.
